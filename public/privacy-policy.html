Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 29, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how our Authentication Microservice ("we", "our", or "the service") collects, uses, and protects your personal information when you use our authentication services through third-party providers (Google, Facebook, and Microsoft).

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information Provided by OAuth Providers

When you authenticate using Google, Facebook, or Microsoft, we receive the following information from the respective provider:

  • User ID: A unique identifier assigned by the OAuth provider
  • Display Name: Your name as shown in your provider account
  • Email Address: Your email address associated with the provider account
  • Profile Photo: Your profile picture (if available from the provider)
  • Provider Information: Which service you used to authenticate (Google, Facebook, or Microsoft)

1.2 Session Information

We create a session when you successfully authenticate. This session includes:

  • Session ID stored in an encrypted cookie
  • Your authentication status
  • Basic user profile information (as listed above)

1.3 Technical Information

We may automatically collect certain technical information, including:

  • IP address
  • Browser type and version
  • Device information
  • Access timestamps

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the collected information for the following purposes:

  • Authentication: To verify your identity and provide secure access to our services
  • Session Management: To maintain your login session and remember your authentication status
  • Service Improvement: To analyze usage patterns and improve our authentication service
  • Security: To detect and prevent fraudulent activity, unauthorized access, and security threats
  • Communication: To respond to your inquiries and provide customer support

3. Data Storage and Security

3.1 Storage

Your authentication information is stored in server-side sessions. By default, session data is stored in memory. We do not permanently store your personal information unless you explicitly request it or it is necessary for the service to function.

3.2 Security Measures

We implement various security measures to protect your information:

  • Encrypted session cookies
  • Secure HTTPS connections (in production)
  • Regular security updates and patches
  • Secure session management practices
  • Access controls and authentication mechanisms
Important: While we take reasonable measures to protect your information, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security of your data.

4. Third-Party Services

Our authentication service relies on third-party OAuth providers. When you authenticate, you are subject to their respective privacy policies:

  • Google: Google Privacy Policy
  • Facebook: Facebook Data Policy
  • Microsoft: Microsoft Privacy Statement

We encourage you to review these policies to understand how these providers handle your information.

5. Cookies and Session Management

We use session cookies to maintain your authentication state. These cookies:

  • Are essential for the authentication service to function
  • Are encrypted and secure
  • Expire when you log out or after a period of inactivity
  • Do not contain sensitive personal information

You can control cookies through your browser settings, but disabling cookies may prevent the authentication service from working properly.

6. Data Retention

We retain your session information only for as long as necessary to provide the authentication service. Specifically:

  • Session data is deleted when you log out
  • Session data expires after a period of inactivity
  • We do not retain your information after your session ends unless required by law

7. Your Rights and Choices

You have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Access: You can request access to the personal information we hold about you
  • Correction: You can request correction of inaccurate information
  • Deletion: You can request deletion of your information by logging out
  • Withdrawal: You can revoke access to your OAuth provider account at any time
  • Opt-out: You can choose not to use our authentication service

To exercise these rights, please contact us using the information provided in the "Contact Us" section below.

8. Children's Privacy

Our authentication service is not intended for children under the age of 13 (or the applicable age of consent in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe we have collected information from a child, please contact us immediately.

9. International Data Transfers

Your information may be transferred to and processed in countries other than your country of residence. These countries may have data protection laws that differ from those in your country. By using our service, you consent to the transfer of your information to these countries.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by:

  • Updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy
  • Posting a notice on our service (if applicable)

We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically to stay informed about how we protect your information.

11. Compliance

We strive to comply with applicable data protection laws, including:

  • General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) for users in the European Union
  • California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) for users in California
  • Other applicable local data protection laws
